Garage & Shop Builder in Grand County, CO

Toys, trucks, and tools need real shelter at 8,000 feet. We build garages and shops the same way we build houses: engineered, insulated, and made to last.

A Garage Is a Small Building, Not a Big Shed

In Grand County a garage carries the same snow load as your house and sits on the same freeze-thaw ground. Skimp on the footings or the roof structure and it shows within a few winters.

We build garages and shops with the full house treatment: excavation and frost-depth footings, a proper slab, engineered framing, and roofing and siding matched to your home — because we self-perform every one of those trades.

What We Build

Most of our garage projects match the main house — same siding, same roofline logic, same trim — so the property reads as one design rather than a house with a box next to it.

  • Attached and detached garages, single to oversized multi-bay
  • Heated shops and workspaces with in-slab radiant heat
  • RV and toy storage with tall doors and reinforced slabs
  • Outbuildings and equipment sheds
  • Garage conversions and additions to existing homes

Permits and Site Work Included

Detached buildings still need permits, setback checks, and inspections in Grand County, and mountain lots usually need real dirt work before anything goes vertical. We handle all of it — the same excavation, concrete, and framing crews that build our custom homes build your garage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you build a heated garage or shop?

Yes — in-slab radiant heat poured with the foundation is the most comfortable and efficient way to heat a mountain shop, and insulating walls and doors correctly matters just as much. We build the whole package.

Will the garage match my existing house?

That's our default. We match siding, roofing, trim, and rooflines so the new building looks like it was always part of the property.

Do detached garages need permits in Grand County?

Almost always, yes — and setbacks and snow-shed paths matter on mountain lots. We handle the permit, engineering, and inspections as part of the project.
