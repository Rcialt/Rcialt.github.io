Siding & Exterior Finishes in Grand County

High-altitude sun, wind-driven snow, and 100-degree seasonal swings are brutal on a house's skin. We install exteriors that keep their looks.

Get a Free Quote

The Exterior Is the House People See

Siding does two jobs: it protects the structure from mountain weather, and it's the first thing anyone — guest, neighbor, or future buyer — judges the house by. We take both jobs seriously.

Our crews install the mountain-modern and classic looks you see across our builds: dark board-and-batten with corrugated wainscot, clean lap siding with black-trimmed windows, cedar shake gables, and heavy timber accents at entries and porches.

Built for This Climate

At 8,000+ feet, UV exposure is fierce, humidity swings crack cheap material, and wind finds every bad seam. Material choice and installation detail matter more here than almost anywhere.

We favor fiber cement and engineered wood products that hold paint and shrug off freeze-thaw, installed over a proper weather barrier with flashed penetrations — so the wall system sheds water for decades, not just past the warranty.

  • Board-and-batten, lap, and shake siding
  • Fiber cement and engineered wood systems
  • Heavy timber posts, brackets, and accents
  • Metal wainscot and accent panels
  • Full house-wrap, flashing, and trim packages
  • Exterior refresh and re-side projects

Re-Siding an Existing Home

A re-side is the fastest way to transform a dated mountain house — and the right time to fix what's hiding underneath. Because we're a full general contractor, we repair sheathing, upgrade the weather barrier, and correct flashing while the walls are open, instead of nailing new siding over old problems.

Frequently Asked Questions

What siding holds up best in Grand County?

Fiber cement and quality engineered wood are our go-to materials — they hold paint, resist UV, and handle freeze-thaw. The bigger factor is installation: weather barrier, flashing, and clearances done right matter more than the brand on the box.

Can you re-side my house without a full remodel?

Yes. Re-siding is a standalone project we do often, and it's the ideal moment to fix sheathing, wrap, and flashing issues while everything is open.

Do you do the timber accents and porch details too?

Yes — timber posts, brackets, gable accents, and porch details are built by our own crews, matched to the framing underneath so they're structural where they need to be.
