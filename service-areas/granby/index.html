Building at 7,935 Feet

Granby sits at 7,935 feet in the Middle Park basin and picks up 128+ inches of snow a year. Roof snow loads here can exceed 200 pounds per square foot, and frost lines run deep. That changes how we engineer everything: foundation depth, truss design, insulation, and ventilation.

We build homes that hold up to it — ice-dam prevention, radon mitigation, heat recovery ventilation, and exterior finishes that last in cold, dry mountain air.

  • Snow-load engineering for high-altitude roofs
  • Deep, frost-protected foundations
  • Radon mitigation and HRV systems built into the design

Where We Build in Granby

We work throughout Granby Ranch, historic downtown along Highway 40, Pole Creek Valley, and rural properties out toward Lake Granby and the Arapaho National Forest. Many sites sit 5 to 10 miles from town on county roads or private drives — we plan equipment access and delivery around seasonal road conditions from day one.

Granby is 85 miles northwest of Denver, 15 minutes from Winter Park, and 10 minutes from Hot Sulphur Springs. If your lot is anywhere in that stretch, we've probably built near it.

Permits, Septic, and Site Work

The Town of Granby and Grand County have specific setback rules, septic standards, and wildlife protection zones. We file the permits, handle septic approval and fire marshal clearance, and track inspections through final walkthrough.

Because we're also an excavating contractor, site prep, grading, drainage, and septic installation happen on our schedule — not a subcontractor's.
