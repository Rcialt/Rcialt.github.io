River Valley Terrain

Kremmling sits where the Blue River and Muddy Creek meet the Colorado River, between canyons at the lower, more open end of Grand County. Lots here often need grading, drainage planning, and careful well placement before a foundation goes in — work we handle with our own excavation equipment.

The climate is still mountain climate. Roof load ratings, insulation specs, and HVAC capacity all get engineered for heavy snow and cold winters.

Town Lots, Subdivisions, and Ranch Land

Inside town limits, homes connect to city water and sewer, and tighter setbacks call for smart, compact designs with walkable access to the Town Square. Some zoning districts require a Use by Special Review — we handle that application and the Planning & Zoning coordination.

Rural subdivisions like Big Horn Park, Blue Valley Acres, and Grand Cliffs run on private wells and septic, with their own HOA covenants and design rules. We test soils, design compliant systems with county health, and review every restriction before design starts.

  • Well and septic design, testing, and installation
  • HOA covenant and special-review navigation
  • Grand County Building & Sanitation inspections at every phase

Close Enough to Be There

Kremmling is on Highway 40 between Summit County and Steamboat Springs, about 25 minutes from our Granby base. Being that close means real site visits, fast responses, and supplier relationships that already serve this end of the county.
