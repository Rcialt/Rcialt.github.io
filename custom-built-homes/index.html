Mountain-Ready Design and Construction

Granby sits at nearly 8,000 feet with over 128 inches of annual snow. We build homes engineered for exactly that: framing that meets the town's snow load calculations, roof systems that shed heavy snow, foundations that account for seasonal ground movement, and materials that resist UV exposure and big temperature swings.

A home built properly for these conditions performs better and lasts longer. One built to lowland standards starts failing early.

Our Build Process

Every custom home moves through the same clear phases, and you always know what happens next.

We start with design and planning: your vision, the site conditions, and Granby's code requirements. Then we prepare and submit permits, manage excavation and foundation work with elevation certification, frame to snow load and seismic standards, install and test all systems and finishes, and coordinate final inspections through occupancy.

  • Design and planning with feasibility and code review
  • Permits and approvals through town or county
  • Foundation and framing to Granby snow load standards
  • Systems and finishes installed and inspected
  • Final inspection and handoff

Local Expertise and Compliance

We know Granby's planning office, building department, and permit process. We understand what inspectors require, which details need engineer sign-off, and how to handle foundation elevation certification. Contractors unfamiliar with these requirements often face delays or rework.

That knowledge comes from 35+ years of building custom homes, including direct experience with Granby's town approval process.

Where We Build

We have built throughout Granby's residential areas, including Granby Ranch, Grand Elk, and in-town lots. Each has its own approval path: Granby Ranch and Grand Elk add HOA design guidelines on top of town requirements, while in-town lots often mean existing utilities and tighter footprints.

We also build in Fraser, Grand Lake, Winter Park, and across Grand County. These communities share Granby's elevation and snow demands, and we bring the same attention to code compliance and site-specific design wherever your lot is.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes building in Granby different from other Colorado towns?

The elevation (7,935 feet), heavy snowfall (128+ inches annually), and seismic design requirements mean foundations, framing, and roofs must be engineered specifically for these conditions. The town also requires elevation certification and careful site planning, which we manage start to finish.

What is foundation elevation certification?

The Town of Granby requires an Improvement Location Certificate and Foundation Setback and Elevation Certification before framing begins. It proves your foundation meets setback rules and elevation standards. We prepare and submit it with town planning staff.

Can I build on mountain land outside town limits?

Yes. Properties outside Granby town limits fall under Grand County regulations instead. We handle both town and county permitting and will clarify which jurisdiction applies to your property.

Do I need a licensed architect for my custom home?

For simpler projects, our team can prepare the drawings. Complex designs and certain structural elements require a Colorado-licensed architect or engineer to stamp plans, and we coordinate with qualified designers we have worked with before.

What building codes apply in Granby?

Granby has adopted the 2021 International Building Code with local amendments, including seismic design category B, specific snow load calculations, and flood damage prevention standards. We build to all of them.
