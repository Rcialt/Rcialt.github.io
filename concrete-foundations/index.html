Foundations Are Where Houses Are Won or Lost

Every crack in a wall, every sticking door, every out-of-level floor traces back to what happened below grade. In Grand County, foundations fight rocky ground, expansive soils in places, and a freeze-thaw cycle that runs half the year.

Because we excavate, form, and pour with our own people, nothing gets lost between subs: the over-dig is right, the drainage is planned before the first form goes up, and the mud goes in on schedule instead of waiting weeks for a concrete crew's next opening.

Concrete Work We Do

From full custom-home foundations to garage slabs, our concrete work is engineered for this elevation: footers below frost depth (30+ inches here), reinforced walls, and mixes and curing practices matched to mountain temperature swings.

  • Footers and frost-protected foundations
  • Poured foundation walls and crawlspaces
  • Garage, shop, and basement slabs
  • Driveways, walks, and exterior flatwork
  • Foundation drainage, waterproofing, and backfill
  • ILC and foundation elevation certifications coordinated

Timing the Pour in the Mountains

Concrete season and excavation season overlap tightly here, and a foundation that goes in late can strand a build until spring. Running dirt work and concrete under one roof lets us sequence the dig, the pour, and the cure inside the weather window — it's one of the biggest schedule advantages we offer.

Frequently Asked Questions

How deep do foundations need to be in Grand County?

Footers must sit below frost depth — commonly 30 inches or more depending on your site and the county's requirements. We dig, form, and inspect to the engineered plans for your specific lot.

Do you pour concrete in winter?

The practical season runs roughly May through October. Cold-weather pours are possible with blankets and additives when a project demands it, but we plan sequencing so your foundation goes in inside the window whenever we can.

Can you replace or repair a failing foundation?

Yes. Settled corners, cracked walls, and water intrusion are all fixable — we assess the cause first (usually drainage or shallow footings), then engineer the repair rather than patching the symptom.
