Built for Granby, Not a Stock Plan

A custom home in Granby starts with your needs and the land itself. We design and build homes that frame the mountain views, carry the snow loads, and hold their efficiency in Colorado's thin air. Every project is built to your specifications, not a floor plan pulled off a shelf.

Granby sits at nearly 8,000 feet and gets over 128 inches of snow a year. That means high-performance insulation, roof pitch that sheds snow, and materials proven against freeze-thaw and UV. After 35+ years as builders, we know what works in this climate and what fails.

Custom Built Homes

We handle the full build: design coordination, site preparation, foundation, framing, mechanical and electrical systems, interior finishes, and final inspections. You pick the materials, finishes, and layout. We make sure it all meets code and holds up to mountain winters.

Your land, your design, your timeline. Custom building gives you control that spec homes cannot, whether you are building a primary residence in town or a mountain retreat on county land.

  • Design and planning with your input and local expertise
  • Site preparation and foundation adapted to mountain terrain
  • Quality construction with ongoing inspections and communication

Project Management on Every Build

A dedicated project manager coordinates contractors, suppliers, inspections, and permits so you are not juggling dozens of moving parts. You get regular updates, transparent cost tracking, and one point of contact from foundation to closeout.

That structure is what keeps a Granby build on schedule through weather delays, material lead times, and inspection checkpoints. No surprises at the end.

Local Codes and Permits, Handled

We obtain building permits from the Town of Granby or Grand County depending on where your lot sits, schedule every inspection, and manage code compliance through the whole build. We know the local planning offices and what inspectors expect.

That familiarity prevents the delays and rework that catch builders who do not work here regularly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need permits for custom home construction in Granby?

Yes. All new residential construction requires building permits, from the Town of Granby for in-town lots or from Grand County for properties outside town limits. We obtain the permits, schedule inspections, and handle code compliance throughout the build.

How does the mountain climate affect home building in Granby?

High elevation and heavy snow require reinforced roof design, quality insulation, proper drainage, and materials rated for big temperature swings. We build to Granby's snow load calculations and choose materials that hold up to freeze-thaw cycles and intense UV.

Can I make changes during construction?

Design changes are easiest in the early phases. Later changes can affect cost and schedule, so your project manager reviews timing, feasibility, and impact with you before any modification is approved.

Why does project management matter on a custom build?

It keeps trades coordinated, materials ordered on schedule, and inspections tracked. That is what prevents delays, cost overruns, and quality problems, and it gives you one accountable contact for the entire project.
