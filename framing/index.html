The Trade We Built the Company On

Before Rondeau Construction was a custom home builder, we were a framing crew. Measuring twice, cutting once, showing up early. Thirty-five years later, every project we run still starts with that framing mentality: get the structure right and everything else follows.

A weak frame doesn't show up on day one. It shows up as cracks, sagging ridgelines, sticking doors, and drywall seams that open every winter. We've spent three decades making sure that never happens to a house with our name on it.

Framing for Grand County Conditions

At 8,000 feet, framing is engineering. Roof structures here carry snow loads of 100 pounds per square foot and more, and lumber goes through humidity and temperature swings that will find every shortcut.

We frame to current code with engineered plans: correctly sized headers and beams, proper bearing and load paths, straight walls, and roof systems built for the snow this valley actually gets — not the snow a lowland span table assumes.

  • Full structural framing for new custom homes
  • Additions, garages, and shop buildings
  • Engineered roof systems rated for mountain snow loads
  • Timber accents, exposed beams, and porch framing
  • Structural repairs and sistering for older homes

Why It Matters for Your Build

Because we frame with our own crew instead of subcontracting it out, the schedule doesn't slip waiting on someone else, and the quality is checked by the same people who will hand the house to you at the final walkthrough. Plumb, level, and square isn't a slogan — it's what makes every trade after us faster and cleaner.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you do framing-only jobs, or just full builds?

Both. We frame our own custom homes, and we also take on framing packages for additions, garages, and structural repairs throughout Grand County.

What snow load do you frame roofs for in Grand County?

It depends on elevation and location — roof snow loads in our area commonly run 100 pounds per square foot and higher. We frame to the engineered plans and current county code for your specific site, not a generic span table.

Can you fix framing problems in an older home?

Yes. Sagging ridgelines, undersized beams, and settled floors are common in older mountain homes. We assess the structure, engineer the fix, and do the repair with our own crew.
