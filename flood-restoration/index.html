Flood Restoration in Granby, CO

Spring runoff, mountain storms, or a burst pipe: we extract the water, dry the structure, and rebuild before mold gets a foothold.

Get a Free Quote

Water Extraction and Drying

Standing water needs to come out within hours, not days. We deploy industrial pumps, wet vacuums, and dehumidifiers to pull water from basements, crawlspaces, and finished areas.

Drying equipment runs continuously until moisture readings confirm the structure is safe. That is what prevents the warping, buckling, and decay that follow spring runoff events in mountain communities like Granby.

Contamination and Mold Prevention

Floodwater carries sewage, chemicals, and bacteria. We remove contaminated drywall, insulation, and flooring, then apply EPA-approved disinfectants to every surface the water touched.

Speed matters here: wet insulation and wood framing can start growing mold within 24 to 48 hours. Materials that soak cannot be dried in place safely, so we remove them and stop mold colonies before they start.

Reconstruction and Restoration

Once the property is dry and clean, we rebuild. Drywall, flooring, cabinetry, and fixtures go in to code and matched to your home's original finish.

We pull Grand County permits where structural, electrical, or HVAC work requires them, and coordinate the inspections. Because we are a full general contractor, the same team that dried your home rebuilds it.

Insurance and Documentation

We photograph all damage, track every step of the restoration, and provide itemized reports for your insurance company. Clear documentation strengthens claims and speeds reimbursement.

Note that standard homeowners policies do not cover flood damage; only a separate NFIP or private flood policy does. Either way, we document everything so you can recover the maximum your coverage allows.

Built for Grand County's Water Problems

Mountain runoff, spring thaw, high groundwater, and sump pump failures are the usual culprits here, and basements take the worst of it. We address the root cause, not just the symptoms, adding drainage, grading, or sump systems where needed to prevent a repeat.

We serve Granby and the broader Grand County region, including Fraser, Grand Lake, and Tabernash.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do immediately after a flood in my home?

Get to safety first, then call us for extraction and assessment. While you wait: shut off electricity if it is safe to do so, stay out of contaminated water, open windows for air circulation, and photograph the damage for your insurance company.

Will my homeowners insurance cover flood damage?

Standard homeowners policies do not cover flood damage. Only a separate National Flood Insurance Program policy or private flood insurance covers it. Check with your agent right away; burst-pipe water damage is often treated differently than flooding.

Can drywall and insulation be saved after water exposure?

No. Drywall and fiberglass insulation absorb water and can become mold colonies within 24 to 48 hours. They must be removed and replaced, not dried in place.

What happens if I wait instead of calling right away?

Mold starts within hours, and wood framing softens as it absorbs moisture, causing long-term structural damage. Professional drying costs far less than the rot repair, mold remediation, and frame replacement that follow a delay.

Do you handle basement floods?

Yes. Basements are the most common flood zone in mountain homes due to groundwater, sump pump failure, and spring runoff. We pump out the water, dry the foundation walls, remove contaminated materials, and fix the root cause.
