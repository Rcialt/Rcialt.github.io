Fire Mitigation Services in Granby, CO

We clear defensible space, remove hazardous fuel, and harden homes against embers before wildfire season arrives.

Get a Free Quote

Defensible Space Creation

Defensible space is your first line of defense. We clear dead vegetation, thin tree crowns, and remove lower branches to create zones that slow fire spread around your home.

Our work follows the three standard zones: the immediate perimeter (0-5 feet from structures), the intermediate zone (5-30 feet), and the extended zone (30-100+ feet). Each has specific requirements, and we document compliance in every one.

  • Immediate zone (0-5 ft): cleared of vegetation and dead debris
  • Intermediate zone (5-30 ft): thinned trees, raised canopies, minimal undergrowth
  • Extended zone (30-100+ ft): selective removal to reduce fuel density

Roof and Siding Hardening

Embers travel miles ahead of visible flames and lodge in gutters, under eaves, and behind siding. We seal those vulnerable points and coordinate roof or siding repairs that cut ember intrusion risk.

Common hardening work includes clearing gutters, installing gutter guards, sealing soffit vents, upgrading roof vents with 1/8-inch mesh, and replacing damaged fascia. For homes near the national forest boundaries around Granby, these upgrades directly improve survivability.

Brush and Debris Removal

Accumulated brush and dead wood are fuel. We remove dead trees, cut standing snags, clear undergrowth, and either haul debris to local disposal facilities or chip it on-site, whichever you prefer.

We work carefully around structures and landscaping, removing hazardous material while preserving the healthy trees and character of your land. Everything removed is documented, which helps with insurance.

Why Mitigation Pays Off

Wildfire risk in Grand County has increased, and cleared perimeters with sealed vents make a measurable difference in whether a structure survives when fire conditions arrive.

Many insurers offer premium discounts or survivability credits for documented mitigation work. Check with your insurer before work begins; we provide detailed reports of everything completed so you can claim available discounts.

Our crews live here, know the terrain and tree species, and follow Firewise principles and county guidelines on every property.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a permit for fire mitigation work in Granby?

Most residential mitigation work does not require a permit, but we check local requirements and notify the county when needed for your specific property.

Will mitigation work ruin my landscaping?

No. We preserve healthy trees and ornamental plantings where possible. The goal is to thin density and remove hazardous material while keeping your landscape's character.

How do I know if my property meets defensible space standards?

We assess and photograph all three zones and provide a report documenting compliance with Firewise and county defensible space guidelines.

What happens to the debris?

We haul it to local disposal facilities or chip it on-site, depending on your preference and property conditions. Nothing is left behind.

Can you handle the roof and siding repairs too?

Yes. As a licensed general contractor we can do hardening repairs ourselves rather than referring them out, which keeps the whole mitigation scope with one team.
