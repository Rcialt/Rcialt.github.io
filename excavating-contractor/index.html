Excavation Done Right From the Start

Excavation is the first step in any major project, and in Granby the soil, rock, and elevation changes make it a step worth getting right. We remove overburden, grade building pads, and manage water runoff to protect your project from day one.

Poor site preparation leads to foundation cracks, settling, and expensive repairs later. We assess drainage patterns, check soil stability, and grade to your home's design so problems never get a chance to start.

Excavation Services in Granby

We handle site clearing, foundation excavation, grading, and earthwork with our own equipment and operators who work mountain terrain year in and year out. Precision grading prevents water pooling and keeps runoff moving away from foundations.

We work within Granby's excavation season and permit requirements, and we coordinate directly with local inspectors so your project moves to the next phase without delay.

  • Site clearing and grading for foundations
  • Building pad preparation and drainage design
  • Utility trenching and earthwork

Septic System Installation

Most rural and mountain properties around Granby rely on septic systems. Installation here takes soil testing, correct depth calculation, and compliance with Grand County health codes, and we handle all of it.

Freezing temperatures and rocky ground affect how a system performs at this elevation. We position tanks and drainfields for durability and reliable operation through deep-frost winters.

How We Work

Every job follows the same sequence: assess the site, set up equipment safely, excavate and grade to plan, then walk the final grades with the inspector.

We evaluate soil type, drainage, grade, and code requirements before a machine ever moves dirt. That upfront work is what keeps schedules and budgets intact.

  • Site assessment: soil, drainage, grade, and code review
  • Equipment setup that protects surrounding property
  • Excavation and grading with proper drainage paths
  • Inspection and signoff coordinated with local officials

Frequently Asked Questions

What does an excavating contractor do for a new home in Granby?

We clear the site, remove topsoil and rock, grade the building pad, and establish proper drainage before foundation work begins. Done right, this prevents settlement and water problems for the life of the home.

Does Granby's elevation affect excavation work?

Yes. Higher elevation means colder winters, rockier soil, and different drainage patterns than the Front Range. We adjust equipment, methods, and grading design to handle Granby's specific conditions.

How do you ensure proper drainage on a building site?

We assess slope and water flow, grade away from foundations, and install swales or ditches where needed to manage runoff and prevent water damage.

What permits are required for excavation and septic work in Granby?

Excavation and grading permits are required by the Town of Granby, and septic work requires Grand County health department permits. We handle the applications and coordinate with local inspectors.

Why is septic installation such a big deal here?

Most Granby-area properties are on septic. Proper installation means reliable waste treatment for decades and protects the groundwater this mountain community depends on.
