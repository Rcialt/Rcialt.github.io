Remodeling for Mountain Homes

Whole home remodeling in Granby means dealing with the realities of mountain living: weatherproofing aging cabins, updating homes built for earlier decades, and handling framing, electrical, plumbing, and finishes under one team.

Granby sits at nearly 8,000 feet with over 128 inches of annual snow. Freeze-thaw cycles, heavy roof loads, intense UV, and ground movement all shape how we approach a remodel. Upgrades made without those considerations fail early and cost more to fix.

Kitchens and Bathrooms

A kitchen remodel here covers layout, cabinets, countertops, appliances, electrical, plumbing, and flooring, and we design with the mountain view in mind, since Granby kitchens often benefit most from bigger windows and better light. We coordinate gas lines, venting, and utility upgrades for high-altitude performance.

Bathrooms get complete treatment: fixtures, tile, ventilation, plumbing, and waterproofing. Mountain homes demand careful moisture control, so we design drainage and ventilation that handle Granby's snow and humidity cycles, plus heated floors where you want them.

Structural and Framing Work

Framing is our foundation as a company. Whether we are adding a room, opening walls, or replacing damaged wood, we build with code and climate in mind: snow loads, freeze-thaw, and settlement all factor into how we frame.

We also handle deck construction, siding replacement, and roof framing built to stand up to mountain weather.

How a Remodel Runs

Every project follows a clear sequence, and you know what happens next at each stage. We walk your home, document existing conditions, and flag risks upfront. Permits, utility upgrades, and structural surprises are discussed before work starts, not after.

When we find issues during demo, we call and discuss options before proceeding. You get a written scope, a materials list, and Grand County permit requirements in the estimate.

  • Initial visit and detailed written estimate
  • Site protection, demolition, and utility coordination
  • Framing, structural work, and rough systems with inspections
  • Drywall, paint, flooring, trim, cabinetry, and fixtures
  • Final inspections and owner walkthrough

One Team, Full Responsibility

We do not subcontract everything out. Our crews frame, manage systems, and oversee finishes, which means accountability, consistency, and faster communication. You work with one project manager and one company.

We serve homes throughout Grand County: downtown Granby, Winter Park, Grand Lake, Kremmling, and the ranches scattered across the valley, from historic homes to newer construction.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need permits for a whole home remodel in Granby?

Yes. Structural, electrical, plumbing, and mechanical work all require permits. We handle the permitting process, inspections, and code compliance as part of our scope.

What about snow load and mountain building codes?

Mountain codes require larger structural members, steeper roof pitches, and foundations designed for Granby's snow and altitude. We design and build to those requirements the first time; cutting corners on mountain codes leads to damage and liability.

Can you work with my insurance claim for fire or flood damage?

Yes. We document damage, provide detailed restoration estimates, and coordinate directly with your insurance company. We have handled many fire and flood claims in Grand County.

What if my home has asbestos or lead paint?

Older Granby homes sometimes contain both. We identify these materials upfront, bring in licensed removal specialists, and handle disposal to code before other work proceeds.

How do payments work?

We accept progress payments as work advances and can work with whatever financing you arrange, whether a home equity line, bank loan, or cash. We set the payment schedule with you during the initial estimate.
