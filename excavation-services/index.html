Excavation Services in Granby, CO

Foundation excavation, grading, and land clearing done with the right equipment for mountain soil, groundwater, and steep terrain.

Get a Free Quote

Know the Site Before the Dig

Every job starts with a complete site evaluation. Granby sits at nearly 8,000 feet with heavy seasonal snowfall and groundwater that rises and falls through the year. We assess soil type, drainage patterns, and elevation before planning any cut.

That assessment sets the foundation depth, the grade plan, and the erosion controls. Getting it right up front is why our foundations drain right the first time.

Foundation Excavation and Grading

Foundation excavation for new homes requires precision and knowledge of Granby's soil and groundwater patterns. We remove overburden, establish proper depths, and prepare level bases for foundation work.

Grading and site prep put drainage where it belongs: away from structures and in compliance with Town of Granby setback rules. We handle steep terrain regularly, including the slopes around Granby Hill and Granby Ranch properties.

Land Clearing and Site Prep

Before building begins, we remove trees and brush, level uneven terrain, and prepare stable buildable pads while controlling erosion and stormwater runoff.

High-altitude conditions and winter snow loads demand careful slope design and compaction. When we hand off a pad, it is ready for the next construction phase without settlement surprises.

Permits and the Granby Excavation Season

Granby requires grading and excavation permits for significant earth-moving work, and the excavation season runs May 1 to October 15 per town regulations, with emergency-only exceptions outside that window. We plan projects around those dates and handle the permit applications ourselves.

We coordinate with the town's Public Works Department and Building Division and manage the final inspection sign-off, keeping your building permit file complete.

  • Grading and excavation permit applications handled for you
  • Work scheduled within the May 1 to October 15 season
  • Erosion and stormwater controls per Granby environmental standards
  • Final inspection coordinated with the town

Protecting Grand County's Water

Properties near Lake Granby, the Colorado River, and the Fraser River need extra environmental care. We manage runoff and erosion control to Granby standards, using sediment barriers and stormwater controls that protect adjacent properties and waterways.

We work throughout Downtown Granby, West Granby, Granby Hill, Granby Ranch, and Grand Elk, on lots from small residential parcels to larger development projects.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a permit for excavation work on my Granby property?

Yes. The Town of Granby requires a grading or excavation permit for significant earth-moving work before it begins. We handle the applications for you.

When is excavation work allowed in Granby?

The town's excavation season runs May 1 to October 15, unless work qualifies as an emergency. Heavy snow and frozen ground make winter work impractical and typically not permitted.

Can you work sloped terrain like Granby Hill or West Granby?

Yes. Steep slopes require careful grading and erosion control. We design slopes and drainage to prevent soil movement and protect adjacent properties.

How do you manage drainage near the Fraser River and Lake Granby?

We follow Granby environmental standards and Colorado water regulations: stormwater management, erosion control, and sediment barriers, with inspections confirming compliance.

What equipment do you use?

Modern heavy equipment suited to high-altitude, mountain terrain, from compact machines for tight lots to large excavators for major site prep.
